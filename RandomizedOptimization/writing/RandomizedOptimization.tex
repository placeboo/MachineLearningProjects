\section{Four-Peaks Problem}
The four-peak problem operates on string bit $s$. The goal is to maximize a fitness function \ref{eq: 4-peak-fitness} based on the consecutive number of 1s in the string and thresholds $R$. 
\begin{align}
    f(s, R) = & \max{\{T(s), H(s)\}} \nonumber \\
    &+ \mid s \mid \cdot \mathbb{I}\{(H(s) > R) \land (T(s) > R)\}\label{eq: 4-peak-fitness}
\end{align}
where $T(s)$ is the number of consecutive 1s at the beginning of the string $s$, $H(s)$ is the number of consecutive 1s at the end of the string $s$, $R$ is a thresholds, $ \mid s \mid $ is the length of the string, and $\mathbb{I}$ is the indicator function. There are four "peaks"
as the landscape charecterized by the fitness function: two local optimas and two global optima. For quick understanding, suppose the string length is 8, and the threshold $R$ is 3. \begin{itemize}
    \item Two local optimas: 11111111, 00000000, whose fitness is 8;
    \item Two global optimas: 11110000, 00001111, whose fitness is 12.
\end{itemize}
